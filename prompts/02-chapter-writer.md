# Prompt 2: Chapter Content Generator

## Purpose
Write one complete ebook chapter — from hook to summary — maintaining consistent voice, depth, and practical value throughout.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a professional nonfiction ghostwriter. Write one complete chapter for an ebook with the following context:

**Ebook Title:** [EBOOK_TITLE]
**Target Reader:** [TARGET_READER — who they are, experience level, core pain]
**Chapter Number:** [NUMBER]
**Chapter Title:** [CHAPTER_TITLE]
**Chapter Premise:** [One sentence — what this chapter proves or teaches]
**Key Points to Cover:**
1. [KEY_POINT_1]
2. [KEY_POINT_2]
3. [KEY_POINT_3]
(add more as needed)
**Target Word Count:** [WORD_COUNT — typically 1,500–3,000 words]
**Tone:** [TONE — e.g., "Direct and tactical, like a trusted expert colleague", "Warm and encouraging", "Data-driven and analytical"]
**Previous Chapter Summary:** [PREV_CHAPTER — one sentence on what the last chapter covered, or "N/A" for Chapter 1]

---

Write the following sections in order:

## Chapter [NUMBER]: [CHAPTER_TITLE]

### Opening Hook (150–250 words)
Open with ONE of these techniques (choose the strongest fit):
- A surprising or counterintuitive statistic that challenges a common assumption
- A brief story (real or composite) showing someone in the exact situation your reader is in
- A provocative question that the reader has been asking themselves
- A bold claim that the chapter will prove

Transition from the hook to: "In this chapter, you'll learn..."

### Section 1: [FIRST_KEY_POINT_AS_SUBHEADING]
- 400–600 words
- Define any key terms upfront
- Explain the concept clearly — assume smart reader, not an expert
- Include at least one concrete example, case study, or data point
- End with a "Why This Matters" sentence connecting to the reader's outcome

### Section 2: [SECOND_KEY_POINT_AS_SUBHEADING]
- 400–600 words
- Build on Section 1 — each section should feel like a logical next step
- Include a practical framework, checklist, or step-by-step process where applicable
- Use a real-world scenario or example to illustrate the concept

### Section 3: [THIRD_KEY_POINT_AS_SUBHEADING] (and additional sections as needed)
- 400–600 words per section
- Same structure as above

### Action Exercise
A specific, completable task the reader can do in the next 30 minutes to apply what they just learned. Format:
- **Time required:** [X minutes]
- **What to do:** [Step-by-step instructions, 3–5 steps]
- **What you'll have when done:** [Tangible output]

### Chapter Summary
- 3–5 bullet points recapping the key takeaways (one per major concept)
- One sentence: "Now that you know [CHAPTER_CONCEPT], in the next chapter we'll cover [NEXT_CHAPTER_TOPIC]."

---

**Formatting rules:**
- Use H3 subheadings (###) for sections within the chapter
- Use bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Bold key terms on first use
- Keep paragraphs to 3–5 sentences maximum
- No filler phrases ("In today's world...", "It's important to note that...")
- Write like you're talking to one specific person, not a crowd
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Run this prompt once per chapter — don't try to write multiple chapters in one prompt
- Copy the "Chapter Summary" output from each chapter and paste it as the "Previous Chapter Summary" for the next run
- If you want to adjust voice: "Match the writing style of [AUTHOR NAME]" or "More conversational, less formal"
- For technical topics: "Assume the reader has basic familiarity with [PREREQUISITE] but no experience with [ADVANCED_CONCEPT]"

## Consistency Across Chapters

To maintain consistent voice when writing multiple chapters:
1. After the first chapter, save 2–3 sample paragraphs as your "voice guide"
2. Add at the end of subsequent prompts: "Match this writing style exactly: [PASTE SAMPLE PARAGRAPHS]"

## Customization

- Add callout boxes: "Include 2 'Pro Tip' callout boxes in this chapter highlighting [TOPIC]"
- Add quotes: "Include 1–2 relevant quotes from industry experts"
- Add data: "Reference statistics from [INDUSTRY REPORT] where relevant"
- Adjust depth: "This is a beginner-level ebook — avoid jargon and define all technical terms"
