# Prompt 4: Launch Email Sequence Generator

## Purpose
Write the complete email campaign to promote and deliver your lead magnet — 5 promotional emails to drive downloads, plus a 3-email nurture sequence for new subscribers after they opt in.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an email copywriter specializing in lead magnet launches and subscriber nurture sequences. Write a complete email campaign for the following:

**Lead Magnet Title:** [TITLE]
**Topic Summary:** [TOPIC — 1–2 sentences on what the lead magnet covers]
**Target Audience:** [AUDIENCE — who is on this list or being targeted]
**Biggest Pain This Solves:** [PAIN]
**Key Transformation:** [TRANSFORMATION — what the reader can do after consuming the lead magnet]
**Brand Voice:** [VOICE — e.g., "Direct and tactical", "Warm and encouraging", "Data-driven", "Conversational and witty"]
**Sender Name:** [NAME — how you'll sign the emails]
**What Subscribers Get After Downloading:** [NEXT_STEP — e.g., "added to a welcome sequence for [COURSE/PRODUCT]", "receive weekly newsletter on [TOPIC]", "nothing yet — just the content"]

---

## PART A: 5-Email Promotional Sequence

Write 5 emails to promote the lead magnet to an existing list or warm audience. Send one per day over 5 days.

### Email 1 — The Teaser (Day 1)
**Goal:** Create anticipation, establish the problem
- Subject line: Hint at the content without revealing it; create curiosity gap
- Preview text (50 chars max): Extends the subject line
- Body: 
  - Open with a story, stat, or scenario that perfectly describes the reader's pain
  - Name the problem clearly — make them feel seen
  - Tease that something is coming: "I've been working on something that fixes exactly this..."
  - Soft CTA: "Reply with '[KEYWORD]' and I'll send you the details tomorrow"
- Signature

### Email 2 — The Launch (Day 2)
**Goal:** Drive downloads with full reveal
- Subject line: Direct reveal + benefit ("Your free [TITLE] is ready")
- Preview text: Urgency or social proof element
- Body:
  - Brief callback to yesterday's email
  - The offer: "I just released [TITLE] — here's what's inside:"
  - 5 bullet points (specific benefits, not chapter titles)
  - Primary CTA button: "[DOWNLOAD_NOW_COPY] →"
  - Social proof: downloads, who it's for, one testimonial if available
  - Secondary CTA below signature (plain text link)
- Signature

### Email 3 — The Value-Add (Day 3)
**Goal:** Give a free sample, re-engage non-openers
- Subject line: "Here's a preview of [SPECIFIC_CHAPTER_OR_SECTION]"
- Preview text: Tease the insight inside
- Body:
  - "In case you missed it, [TITLE] is still available"
  - Share one key insight, framework, or excerpt from the lead magnet (~150 words of actual value)
  - Bridge: "This is just one of [NUMBER] frameworks inside — the rest are in the full guide"
  - CTA: "Get the complete guide here →"
- Signature

### Email 4 — The Objection Handler (Day 4)
**Goal:** Address the #1 reason people didn't download yet
- Subject line: Addresses a specific hesitation ("'I don't have time for this' — I hear you")
- Preview text: Challenge the objection
- Body:
  - Name the most common objection directly
  - Reframe it: time/complexity/relevance objection handled in 2–3 sentences
  - Proof point: "It takes [TIME] to read, and most people implement [QUICK_WIN] the same day"
  - Reminder CTA with low-friction copy ("It's free — [CLAIM_IT] →")
- Signature

### Email 5 — The Final Push (Day 5)
**Goal:** Create urgency for remaining non-converters
- Subject line: Final reminder with soft urgency ("Last reminder: [TITLE]")
- Preview text: What they're missing
- Body:
  - Acknowledge this is the last email about this offer
  - Quick recap: one sentence on the pain, one on the transformation
  - If there's real scarcity/urgency, use it here (limited DFY spots, deadline offer). If not, use relevance urgency: "This is most useful if [TIMING_CONTEXT]"
  - Final CTA
  - PS: What's coming next for subscribers who do opt in
- Signature

---

## PART B: 3-Email New Subscriber Nurture Sequence

Write 3 emails for people who just downloaded the lead magnet. Send automatically after opt-in:

### Nurture Email 1 — Delivery + Welcome (Immediately after opt-in)
**Goal:** Deliver the content, set expectations, build relationship
- Subject line: "Here's your [TITLE] + what's next"
- Preview text: Access instructions
- Body:
  - Confirm the download (direct link or access instructions)
  - One-sentence welcome: who you are and why you care about this topic
  - What to do with the content (reading order, which section to start with, etc.)
  - Quick win: "Start with [CHAPTER/SECTION] — you can implement [SPECIFIC_ACTION] today"
  - What to expect from future emails (frequency, content type)
- Signature

### Nurture Email 2 — Value Email (3 days after opt-in)
**Goal:** Build trust with a standalone insight, no pitch
- Subject line: Related insight with curiosity hook
- Preview text: The payoff of the insight
- Body:
  - 100% value, no selling
  - One specific tip, framework, or insight related to the lead magnet topic
  - Written for someone who has consumed part of the content — takes it a step further
  - Soft engagement CTA: "Reply and tell me: which part of the guide are you working on first?"
- Signature

### Nurture Email 3 — The Bridge (7 days after opt-in)
**Goal:** Connect new subscriber to your next offer or deeper engagement
- Subject line: Question-based ("Have you tried [ACTION] yet?")
- Preview text: Hint at the answer
- Body:
  - Brief check-in: "It's been about a week since you downloaded [TITLE]..."
  - Value reinforcement: one result subscribers have gotten (or one they should aim for by now)
  - Natural bridge to next step: paid product, community, booking a call, deeper content
  - CTA: Soft and specific, not salesy
- Signature

---

**Email writing rules:**
- Subject lines under 50 characters where possible (mobile preview)
- Preview text never repeats the subject line — always extends or contrasts
- One primary CTA per email — don't split attention
- Plain text paragraphs + one bold CTA button per email
- PS lines are high-read real estate — use for a secondary hook or social proof
- No spam trigger words in subject lines (free*, guaranteed*, act now*)
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- For Part A, specify your existing list: "This is a cold list / warm email list / social media followers being driven to opt in"
- If you have real testimonials, paste them — the prompt will place them where highest-impact
- For Part B, add: "After nurture Email 3, subscribers will be moved to [LIST/SEQUENCE]" to calibrate the bridge
- For multi-product operators: "The next offer after nurture is [PRODUCT] at [PRICE]"

## Scheduling Guidance

**Promo sequence (Part A):** Send over 5 consecutive business days for maximum impact

**Nurture sequence (Part B):**
- Email 1: Immediately (triggered automation)
- Email 2: 3 days after Email 1
- Email 3: 7 days after Email 1

Then move subscribers to your regular newsletter or evergreen sequence.
