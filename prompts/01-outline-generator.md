# Prompt 1: Ebook Outline Generator

## Purpose
Generate a complete ebook architecture — title, chapter structure, transformation framework, and table of contents — ready to hand to a writer or use as your own writing blueprint.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a professional nonfiction ebook architect. Create a complete ebook outline for the following:

**Topic:** [TOPIC]
**Target Reader:** [TARGET_READER — who they are, their experience level, their biggest pain]
**Desired Outcome:** [DESIRED_OUTCOME — what transformation the reader achieves by the end]
**Format:** [FORMAT — choose one: How-To Guide / Framework / Checklist Collection / Case Study Collection / Reference Guide]
**Approximate Length:** [LENGTH — Short (5,000–8,000 words) / Standard (10,000–15,000 words) / Comprehensive (20,000+ words)]

---

Produce the following:

## 1. Title Options (5 variants)
Generate 5 title + subtitle combinations. Each title should:
- Name the reader's desired outcome directly
- Be specific enough to filter for the right audience
- Follow one of these proven formats:
  - "The [Number]-[Timeframe] [Outcome]"
  - "How to [Achieve Outcome] Without [Common Obstacle]"
  - "[Outcome]: The [Target Reader]'s Guide to [Method]"
  - "The [Descriptive Adjective] Guide to [Topic]"
  - "[Number] Ways to [Achieve Outcome] in [Context]"

## 2. Reader Promise Statement
One paragraph (50–75 words) that appears on the back cover or landing page:
- Who this is for (be specific)
- What they'll be able to do after reading
- What makes this different from generic advice on the topic
- The single biggest transformation they'll experience

## 3. Before/After Transformation Framework
Two columns:
- **Before reading:** 5 bullet points describing the reader's current state (pains, frustrations, false beliefs, lack of knowledge)
- **After reading:** 5 corresponding bullet points describing their transformed state (skills, confidence, outcomes, new beliefs)

## 4. Chapter Architecture (8–12 chapters)
For each chapter, provide:
- **Chapter number and title** (clear, benefit-oriented, not clever for the sake of it)
- **One-sentence premise** (what this chapter proves or teaches)
- **3–5 key points** covered in the chapter
- **Primary takeaway** (the one thing the reader remembers)
- **Recommended word count** (based on complexity)
- **Content type** (Concept + Framework / How-To Steps / Case Study / Checklist / Q&A / Tool Overview)

Structure the chapters as a learning journey:
- Chapters 1–2: Frame the problem, establish credibility, show the reader they're in the right place
- Chapters 3–6: Core methodology/framework — the meat of the book
- Chapters 7–9: Application — how to implement in the reader's specific context
- Final chapter(s): Next steps, common pitfalls to avoid, how to sustain results

## 5. Introduction Outline
- Hook (surprising stat, story, or provocative question)
- Problem statement
- Why existing solutions fail
- What this book offers
- How to use this book
- Quick win promise (what they'll be able to do after Chapter 1)

## 6. Conclusion Outline
- Recap of transformation achieved
- Common mistakes to avoid going forward
- Next steps / what to do in the next 30 days
- Call to action (email list, community, upsell)

## 7. Table of Contents Draft
Formatted, ready to drop into your document. Include chapter numbers, titles, and estimated page ranges.

## 8. Appendix Suggestions
List 2–4 appendix items that would add value (templates, checklists, resource lists, glossary).

---

Tone: Professional but approachable. Assume the reader is intelligent but not an expert on this specific topic. No filler content — every chapter must earn its place.
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- Be specific about your target reader: "freelance graphic designers with 2-5 years experience who are stuck at $50K/year" beats "creative professionals"
- State the format explicitly — a "framework" ebook has a different structure than a "case study collection"
- If you have existing content (blog posts, course material), mention it: "I have 8 existing blog posts on this topic — use them as source material"
- Run this prompt first, review the architecture, then use Prompt 2 for each chapter

## Customization

- Add: "Chapter 3 must include a specific section on [SUBTOPIC]"
- Add: "The tone should match [AUTHOR/BRAND NAME]'s style: [DESCRIBE STYLE]"
- Add: "This ebook will be used as: [use case — lead magnet / paid product / course bonus / conference handout]"
