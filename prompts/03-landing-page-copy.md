# Prompt 3: Landing Page & Opt-In Copy Generator

## Purpose
Write complete conversion-optimized copy for your lead magnet landing page — headline through thank-you page — plus a content upgrade variant for embedding within blog posts.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a direct-response copywriter specializing in lead generation pages. Write complete opt-in landing page copy for the following lead magnet:

**Lead Magnet Title:** [TITLE]
**Lead Magnet Type:** [TYPE — Ebook / Guide / Checklist / Template Pack / Mini-Course / Toolkit]
**Target Reader:** [TARGET_READER — be specific about who this is for]
**Top 3 Benefits:** 
1. [BENEFIT_1 — specific outcome, not vague feature]
2. [BENEFIT_2]
3. [BENEFIT_3]
**Biggest Pain This Solves:** [PAIN — the thing keeping them up at night]
**Social Proof Available:** [PROOF — number of downloads, testimonials, credentials, or "none yet"]
**CTA Action:** [CTA — "Download Free Guide" / "Get Instant Access" / "Send Me the Ebook"]
**Email Platform:** [PLATFORM — ConvertKit / Mailchimp / ActiveCampaign / other — for form copy tone]

---

Produce the following:

## 1. Page Headline Options (5 variants)

Write 5 headlines. For each, specify the conversion angle (curiosity / pain relief / aspiration / specificity / social proof).

Rules:
- Include the reader's desired outcome, not the format of the content
- Be specific — numbers, timeframes, and qualifiers increase conversion
- Avoid generic lead magnet clichés ("Free Guide!", "Download Now!")
- Test variants: one should be a question, one should be bold/contrarian

## 2. Subheadline (2 variants)
Expands on the headline — 1–2 sentences that:
- Confirm the reader is in the right place (calls out your exact audience)
- Reinforces the primary benefit
- Sets up the bullet points below

## 3. Above-the-Fold Section
Complete copy block for everything visible before scroll:
- Recommended headline (pick one from above, justify your choice)
- Subheadline
- Hero benefit statement (10–15 words in large text, like a deck above the bullets)
- 5–7 benefit bullets in the format: "[Verb] [specific outcome] so that [reader result]"
  - Example: "Identify the 3 pricing signals that predict churn — before your customer knows they're leaving"
  - NOT: "Learn about pricing strategies"
- Opt-in form label + placeholder text + CTA button copy (3 variants of button text)
- Trust signal line (below the form — e.g., "No spam. Unsubscribe anytime." or "Join 4,200 [TARGET_READER]s who already downloaded this.")

## 4. Below-the-Fold Section (optional but recommended for longer pages)

### What's Inside
A "sneak peek" section showing the reader what they'll get. Format as a table of contents or chapter preview — 5–8 items, each with a one-line benefit statement.

### Social Proof Block
If social proof exists: format 2–3 testimonials. 
If no social proof yet: write placeholder copy using the "[Number] [TARGET_READER]s have already downloaded this" format with a realistic number for early traction.

### Author/Creator Credibility Block (2–3 sentences)
Establishes why the creator is qualified to write this. Keep it reader-focused: "I've helped 300+ freelancers..." not "I have a degree in..."

### Final CTA
Repeat the opt-in form with a stronger urgency or scarcity message if applicable. No fake urgency — only use if real.

## 5. Thank-You Page Copy
The page the reader lands on after opting in:
- Confirmation headline (acknowledges they made a good decision)
- Delivery instructions (where/how to access the content)
- What to do next (1–3 next steps, building a micro-habit)
- Soft upsell or next offer (optional — one sentence max, only if you have something to offer)

## 6. Content Upgrade Variant
A shorter embed version for use inside a related blog post. Produces:
- Content upgrade box headline (1 line)
- 2–3 benefit bullets
- CTA button text
- Format: [BOX START] ... [BOX END] markers so it's easy to paste into any CMS

---

**Copywriting rules to follow:**
- Write in second person ("you", "your") — direct address throughout
- Specificity beats vagueness — exact numbers, named outcomes, concrete scenarios
- Every line earns its place — cut anything that doesn't advance conversion
- Social proof should name a specific person or role, not just "many happy users"
- Opt-in form copy should reduce friction: "Your best email" > "Email Address"
```

---

## Tips for Best Results

- The more specific you are about your target reader, the better the bullet points
- If you have real testimonials, paste them in — the prompt will format them properly
- For the content upgrade: specify the blog post topic it will appear in for better angle alignment
- Test at least 2 of the 5 headline variants with real traffic before picking a winner

## A/B Testing Priority

From highest to lowest impact:
1. Headline (test first)
2. CTA button text
3. Benefit bullets (number and order)
4. Social proof placement
5. Form field labels

## Customization

- Add: "Brand voice is [DESCRIPTION] — similar to [BRAND EXAMPLE]"
- Add: "This page will be driven by [TRAFFIC SOURCE — cold Facebook ads / warm email list / SEO blog]" — affects urgency and tone
- Add: "Above-the-fold must include a mockup/cover image description" for visual direction
